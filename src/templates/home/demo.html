Social features for visitor's engagement

Doesn't matter how much 'cultural capital' you bring.

The insights from the Visual Feeback Studies inspired us to prototype social strategies which let the visitors engage with their surrounding on more general terms and independently of their cultural capital, in order make them feel more comfortable and valuable to the museum.

Instead of providing people with in-depth (art) historical background information about art works, we designed three app features for location-based, random encounters. One of them lets the visitors of the Rijksmuseum discover hidden stories by other visitors throughout the museum, using one of the new app features on their smartphones.

Rather than following a route, visitors encounter stories coincidentally during their visit and can instantly record and add an observation themselves wherever they like.

By that, each artwork receives a socially augmented layer. How does it work exactly? Watch the trailer from testing our prototypes.
